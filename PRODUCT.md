# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Nic and travel companions (family). Context: on a phone, often standing at a shuttle
pickup point or in transit, frequently in daylight. Job to be done: open the app,
find today's ticket, show the QR/barcode to be scanned, check departure time and
shuttle code. Secondary: spot days where a return ticket hasn't been bought yet.

## Product Purpose

A password-locked static viewer (GitHub Pages) for AO Shuttle e-tickets ingested
automatically from Gmail. Success = the right ticket with a scannable QR is on
screen within seconds of opening the app, with zero manual upkeep.

## Brand Personality

Calm, light, dependable. A quiet personal utility — a ticket wallet, not a dashboard.
Delight lives in small moments (smooth QR swipe, tap feedback, auto-opening the
relevant ticket), never in decoration. Palette anchors to the AO mark: teal accent,
navy-tinted cool neutrals.

## Anti-references

- GitHub-dark dashboard clone (the previous look): heavy borders, saturated blue,
  dense chrome.
- Airline-app maximalism: gradients, promo banners, decorative illustration.
- Anything that slows the "show QR at the curb" path.

## Design Principles

1. **Curbside first** — the phone-in-sunlight scan moment outranks everything;
   light theme is the default, QR stays on pure white.
2. **Chrome disappears** — toolbar and cards recede; departure time, route, and
   warnings are the loudest things on screen.
3. **Motion conveys state** — 150–250 ms ease-out feedback only; the QR swipe is
   the one earned flourish.
4. **Warnings are unmissable but calm** — missing return tickets read at a glance
   without alarm-red panic.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 in both themes; visible focus rings; reduced-motion
alternatives for every animation; tap targets ≥40px in the toolbar and lightbox.
Interface language is Indonesian.
